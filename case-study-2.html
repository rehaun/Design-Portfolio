Research Case Study

What’s included:

Context

In today’s rapidly evolving digital world, AI tools like GPT and Gemini have become essential for professionals and students alike, enabling faster workflows and providing quick solutions to complex tasks. This educational project aims to explore how postgraduate students interact with AI tools, the challenges they face, and their overall perception of these tools in their daily lives. The insights gained will help identify opportunities for improving AI tool experiences.

Business Objectives

Identify opportunities for product development in AI tools like GPT or Gemini. Understand user challenges to create more user-friendly AI solutions.

Research Objectives

Investigate how postgraduate students are currently using AI tools. Understand the barriers they face while interacting with these tools. Identify potential improvements and solutions based on user experiences.

Research Questions

What are the current use cases of AI tools like GPT or Gemini? Are these tools used for general or specialized purposes? Which AI tools do users prefer, how often do they use them, and what alternatives do they turn to when AI tools aren’t available? What problems do users face while interacting with AI tools? How do they overcome these problems? What blockers prevent users from effectively using AI tools? Do users have concerns about using these tools, and how do these concerns influence their experience? Have AI tools significantly changed how people approach work or daily tasks?

Methodology

The research was exploratory, focusing on gathering qualitative data from user opinions. I chose interviews as the method to explore their experiences. The qualitative data was then analyzed using the Affinity Diagram method, which helped identify key patterns and insights.

Target Audience

I chose postgraduate students in computer science, particularly master’s and doctoral candidates abroad, as they frequently engage with AI tools like GPT or Gemini for academic and research tasks. Their advanced use of these tools made them an ideal group for exploring AI’s role in their work. After interviewing six participants, I reached saturation, with recurring patterns and insights emerging from their responses.

Interview Questions

Have you ever used AI tools? What AI tools do you use the most? Can you recall the last time you used an AI tool? In what context did you use it? What did you use before AI tools for similar tasks? Do you mainly use AI tools for tasks similar to the ones you just described? How many times per day do you use AI tools? Why do you use them at these times? What prompts you to use an AI tool in a given situation? What happens right before you decide to use it? Have you ever experienced barriers when using AI tools? What prevented you from starting or completing the task? What do you feel is the main challenge to using AI tools, and how do you typically overcome it? How do you feel about AI tools? Do you have any concerns about them? If you no longer had access to AI tools, how would this impact your work or daily life?

Insights from Interviews

This section presents an analysis of interviews conducted with various users regarding their experiences with generative AI tools. The insights gained are particularly relevant to their professional and academic contexts, providing key data for the design of future products and services.

1. Specialized Use Over General Use

All users reported frequent and varied use of generative AI tools for solving everyday problems, with a notable emphasis on specialized applications. Their usage includes tasks such as paraphrasing texts, analyzing code, and utilizing specialized tools to find and summarize academic papers. One user noted that they rely on AI to summarize articles and surface insights on complex topics, saving significant time. This highlights the need for ease of access and speed in obtaining information.

2. Time Reduction and Enhanced Efficiency

Most users emphasized that AI has significantly reduced the time spent on complex issues — one participant described resolving in a few hours what used to take several days of reading documentation. This demonstrates AI’s potential to enhance efficiency and accelerate work processes.

3. Access and Cost Challenges

Users identified two main barriers to using AI tools: the high costs associated with subscriptions and restrictions from institutions. On the financial side, some users mentioned sharing accounts to make subscriptions more manageable. Others noted that professors or supervisors sometimes discouraged AI use for coursework, limiting adoption despite its benefits — representing an opportunity for products that offer easier, lower-cost access.

4. Concerns and Negative Impacts

Several users expressed concerns about the negative impacts of AI on their learning and skills, describing a feeling of relying on quick fixes rather than truly understanding the material. This concern indicates a need for tools that not only assist in problem-solving but also promote deeper learning.

5. Oversight and Management

While many users acknowledged AI’s ability to provide quick solutions, some raised concerns about accuracy, recalling confident-sounding answers that turned out to be wrong. This highlights the ongoing need for human oversight in decision-making processes.

6. Job Market Changes

Users expressed minor concerns about the impact of AI on future job prospects, but remained confident that human involvement would persist even as ways of working evolve. This perspective suggests that even as AI evolves, the importance of human skills and judgment remains crucial.

Conclusion

This study reveals opportunities for enhancing AI tools like GPT and Gemini for postgraduate students. By addressing user needs and challenges, we can propose targeted solutions to improve the overall user experience. Below are key opportunities and recommendations:

  • Low-Cost Subscription Plans: Offer tiered subscriptions or student discounts to make premium features more accessible.
  • Adaptive Learning AI: Develop models that improve based on individual user feedback, learning from prior interactions.
  • Dynamic Problem-Solving: Enhance the ability of AI to offer step-by-step guidance for complex coding or research issues.
  • Data Visualization Tools: Integrate features that turn AI-generated data into charts or visual aids for presentations or reports.
  • AI-Assisted Peer Review: Introduce peer-review tools to critique and validate academic or technical papers.
  • Collaboration Spaces: Implement shared workspaces for teams to collaborate with AI tools in real-time.
